Tech highlights 2017

Digital currencies go ballistic

Bitcoin has soared almost 20-fold since the start of the year, and in December moved into the mainstream as two major US exchanges began trading futures in the unit

The currency uses peer-to-peer blockchain technology. It has no central bank or government backing, but is "mined" by computers that perform highly complex calculations

Tech highlights 2017

Gene-editing takes further steps forward

Scientists announced in December successful experiments on mice, in alleviating symptoms of kidney disease, diabetes and muscular dystrophy, using CRISPR/Cas9 gene-editing techniques

In July scientists in the US announced first success in repairing a DNA mutation in human embryos

Tech highlights 2017

Artificial Intelligence gets smarter

Alpha Go Zero, descendant of the computer that in 2016 won its first match against a human world champion of the ancient game of Go, thrashes its predecessor 100-0

It took the new AI system 3 days to master the game, by playing against itself

Tech highlights 2017

An unprecedented global cyberattack

The 'Wannacry' virus is unleashed in May, hits more than 300,000 victims in scores of countries, including banks, hospitals and government agencies

Targets were locked out of their computers and payment was demanded in Bitcoins. Security researchers pointed to a potential North Korean link

Tech highlights 2017

Paralysed man uses mind-controlled prosthetic

American Bill Kochevar, paralysed from the shoulders down, moves his arm with his own thoughts for the first time in 8 years, researchers announced in March

Implants attached to his brain sent signals to a computer that was taught to interpret his neural activity

Tech highlights 2017

Driverless vehicles are coming to town

In November Britain’s finance minister Philip Hammond announced plans to put driverless cars on UK roads by 2021

In January Paris began its first experiments in driverless busses, using electric vehicles on a bridge connecting two railway stations

Tech highlights 2017

Drones broaden their portfolio

Researchers in December announced they were studying falcon hunting strategies, with a potential benefit being a design for drones that can knockout rogue drones

In September Dubai announced a successful “concept” flight for a driverless hover-taxi service. Authorities anticipate 5 years of testing before getting a service in the air

Tech highlights 2017

Small bio-tech hope against the global plastic waste crisis

Researchers in April published a study that showed that the beeswax munching wax worm had the ability to digest plastic

The study offered hope for biologically inspired technologies that could be used to deal with the annual 300 million tonnes of global plastic pollution
